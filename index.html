Caribbean Music and musicians

Music such as Soca, Dancehall, Calypso and Reggae are the sounds that are popular among the cultural expriences of the Caribbean. People of the Islands of the Caribbean is best know for the type of music that they play in their vehicles, at their work place and in their homes. These types of music have emerged from our diverse Caribbean experiences. Reggae and dancehall music was created in Jamaica, while Calypso and soca, is the music of Trinidad and Tobago, predated reggae as a musical form.

Both dancehall and reggae are sung and played not only regionally but also internationally. Dancehall music are popular know as street music and is express as art form they way artist express their way of life through this knid of music. Whilst reggae is more for roots, reality and culture. Reggae is also know as Lovers music. Everywhere in our region, there are other native music forms. These include Spouge from Barbados, Punta from Belize, Zouk from Haiti, Fra Fra from Suriname and Chutney from Trinidad and Tobago.
